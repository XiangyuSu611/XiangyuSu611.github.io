title: Redesigning this page, again
tags: design · process
---
After a weekend of tinkering, the publications block finally feels like it belongs to the rest of the site instead of fighting it. The trick, as always, was doing **less**: smaller thumbnails, shorter summaries, a quieter palette.

I keep learning the same lesson — *restraint compounds*. Every element you remove makes the ones that remain work harder. A page with three emphatic things is louder than a page with ten timid ones.

> You can tell a good design from a bad one by how much of it you can ignore.

Next up: probably a proper notes section, so I have somewhere to write down small observations like this one without having to open another platform.

title: Reading *The Design of Everyday Things* for the third time
tags: reading · research
---
Some books you read once; others you [live inside](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Design_of_Everyday_Things). Norman's point about **affordances** keeps coming back to me whenever I look at a UI and ask: *what does this invite me to do?*

A flat button that affords nothing, a link styled to look like plain text, a form field with no label — these are not minimal; they are **opaque**. Minimalism is about removing the unnecessary, not the necessary.
